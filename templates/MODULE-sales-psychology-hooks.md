# MODULE: Sales Psychology Hooks & Openers Library

> **Master reference.** Every voice AI agent prompt should pull from these frameworks.
> These are the hooks, openers, psychological triggers, and conversational weapons
> that keep people on the phone, open them up, and move them toward yes.
> Built from: Hormozi, Belfort, Sandler, SPIN Selling, Cialdini, Oren Klaff, Chris Voss, Grant Cardone, Daniel Pink, Zig Ziglar, Jim Camp, and 100K+ calls on RizzDial.

## PART 1 — THE FIRST 7 SECONDS (Win or Die)

The first 7 seconds determine whether they stay or hang up. Every opener must do THREE things:
1. **Pattern interrupt** — say something unexpected so their brain can't auto-reject
2. **Identity confirmation** — make them say "yes" (micro-commitment)
3. **Curiosity hook** — give them a reason to keep listening

### Speed-to-Lead Openers (They Just Filled Out a Form)

**The "Test Me" Hook (RizzDial Signature)**
~"Hey {{first_name}}, this is {{agent_name}} with {{company_name}}. You just inquired — you wanted to test me out. How do I sound so far?"
→ They laugh or say "good" → micro-commitment achieved → they're IN.
→ Then: "Cool. Give me 43 seconds and I'm gonna ask you 3 quick questions, tell you exactly how we can help, and by the end you'll know if this is worth 15 more minutes of your time. Sound good?"

**The "You're Fast" Hook**
~"Hey {{first_name}}, this is {{agent_name}} — you literally just hit submit like 45 seconds ago, so I wanted to catch you while you're still thinking about it. Quick question before anything else — what made you fill that out right now?"

**The "I'm Not Gonna Sell You" Hook**
~"Hey {{first_name}}, this is {{agent_name}} with {{company_name}}. Real quick — I'm not calling to sell you anything. You filled out a request and I just want to make sure I connect you with the right person. Can I ask you like two questions to figure out who that is?"

**The "Honest Curiosity" Hook**
~"Hey {{first_name}}, this is {{agent_name}}. Before I say anything else — what's going on in your business right now that made you reach out today?"

**The "Speed Flex" Hook**
~"Hey {{first_name}} — you submitted that form about 60 seconds ago. Most companies take 37 minutes to call you back. We took one. That alone should tell you something about how we operate. Got a quick sec?"

### No-Show Recovery Openers

**The "No Blame" Hook**
~"Hey {{first_name}}, this is {{agent_name}} with {{company_name}}. Listen, life happens — I'm not calling to guilt-trip you. I just wanted to check in because most people who book with us do it for a real reason. Is that reason still there?"

**The "Quick Rescue" Hook**
~"Hey {{first_name}}, this is {{agent_name}}. We had a time set and it looks like we missed each other. I'm calling because I've seen people let a missed appointment turn into a missed opportunity, and I didn't want that to happen to you. Got 30 seconds?"

### Database Reactivation / Nurture Openers

**The "Something Changed" Hook**
~"Hey {{first_name}}, this is {{agent_name}} with {{company_name}}. I know we talked a while back and the timing wasn't right. Reason I'm calling is we just launched something new that's actually solving the exact problem you were dealing with — and I thought of you. Got a minute?"

**The "Check-In, Not Chase" Hook**
~"Hey {{first_name}}, this is {{agent_name}}. I'm not calling to pitch you anything — just checking in. Last time we talked you were dealing with {{pain_point}}. Is that still a thing, or did you get it handled?"

## PART 2 — PSYCHOLOGICAL FRAMEWORKS FOR CALL FLOW

### Framework 1: The Time Contract (Hormozi / Belfort)

Before asking questions, give them a specific time commitment and tell them what they get in return. Specific numbers build trust. Round numbers sound made up.

~"Give me 43 seconds and I'll tell you exactly how we can help."
~"I need 2 minutes and 3 questions. By the end, you'll know if this is worth your time."
~"This call is gonna be 90 seconds, tops. I need to ask you 3 things, and then either I connect you with someone who can help, or I let you get back to your day. Fair enough?"

**Why 43 seconds, not 45:** Odd numbers feel calculated, not scripted. "43 seconds" sounds like you've done this enough to know exactly how long it takes. "About a minute" sounds like every other cold caller.

### Framework 2: The Permission Close (Sandler)

Never push. Get permission at every stage. Each "yes" is a micro-commitment that makes the final "yes" easier.

Stage gates:
- "Sound good?" → after the time contract
- "Fair enough?" → after explaining what you'll ask
- "Make sense?" → after sharing an insight
- "Want me to go ahead and..." → before the booking/transfer
- "Cool?" → casual confirmation between sections

### Framework 3: SPIN Discovery (Neil Rackham)

When qualifying, move through this sequence:
1. **Situation** — What's happening now? (current state facts)
2. **Problem** — What's not working? (surface pain)
3. **Implication** — What does that cost you? (amplify pain with real dollars)
4. **Need-payoff** — What would it look like if we fixed that? (make them sell themselves)

Example:
- S: "How many leads are you getting per month right now?"
- P: "And how many of those actually turn into appointments?"
- I: "So if you're getting 200 leads and converting 5%... at a $3K average deal... that's $570K a year in leads that go nowhere. Does that math sound about right?"
- N: "If we could double that conversion rate — even just from 5% to 10% — what would that do for your business?"

### Framework 4: Loss Aversion (Kahneman / Cialdini)

People are 2x more motivated to avoid losing something than to gain something. Frame everything as what they're LOSING, not what they'd gain.

**Gain frame (weak):** "Our AI can help you book more appointments."
**Loss frame (strong):** "Right now, 80% of the leads you're paying for are dying in your inbox before anyone calls them back. That's money you already spent, walking out the door."

**The Cost of Inaction Math:**
~"Let me ask you something. You said you get about [X] leads a month and close about [Y]%. At [$Z] per deal, that means roughly [$LOSS] per month is slipping through because of follow-up. Over a year, that's [$ANNUAL]. Does that sound about right?"

Always make them confirm the number. When THEY say "yeah, that's about right" — they just sold themselves.

### Framework 5: The Labeling Technique (Chris Voss)

Name what they're feeling before they say it. It builds instant rapport and makes them feel understood.

~"It sounds like you've been burned before."
~"It seems like the follow-up piece is what's really frustrating you."
~"It sounds like you know exactly what you need — you just haven't found anyone who can actually execute it."

### Framework 6: The Takeaway (Sandler / Camp)

Create psychological tension by subtly suggesting this might NOT be for them. Scarcity + qualification = desire.

~"Honestly, this might not even be a fit — which is totally fine. That's what these questions are for."
~"I want to make sure this actually makes sense for your business before we go any further. Not everyone's a good fit and I'd rather tell you that now than waste your time."
~"We only take on [X] new clients a month because of the setup involved. So I want to make sure the numbers work on both sides."

### Framework 7: The "Wow" Moment (RizzDial Signature)

At some point in every call, create a moment where the prospect thinks "holy shit, this is different." It should feel unrehearsed.

For RizzDial agents calling on behalf of RizzDial:
~"By the way — you know you're talking to AI right now, right? ... Yeah. This is actually a demo of what we'd build for your business. Every lead you generate gets a call like this one within 60 seconds. How crazy is that?"

For agents calling on behalf of other businesses:
~"Here's the thing most people don't realize — the leads aren't bad. The follow-up is. 78% of buyers go with whoever responds first. If your team takes 37 minutes and your competitor takes 60 seconds... who do you think gets the deal?"

### Framework 8: The Assumptive Bridge (Belfort)

Don't ask IF they want to proceed. Assume they do and ask about the DETAILS of proceeding.

**Weak:** "Would you like to schedule a call?"
**Strong:** "Alright, so the next step is getting you on the calendar with our team. Are mornings or afternoons usually better for you?"

**Weak:** "Are you interested in learning more?"
**Strong:** "Cool — so what I'm gonna do is connect you with {{rep_name}} who handles exactly this. They'll walk you through the specifics and build your custom plan. Let me get you over to them right now."

## PART 3 — MID-CALL POWER MOVES

### The Controlled Tangent
When the prospect gives a gold nugget (past failure, competitor frustration, urgent deadline), DON'T rush back to the script. Dig for 15-20 seconds. It builds trust and uncovers the real buying motivation.

~"Wait, hold on — back up. You said your last agency was sending you leads that couldn't even qualify. What was happening there?"

### The Expert Pattern Match
Drop a one-liner that proves you've seen this before. Makes them feel like you understand their world.

~"Yeah, that's actually super common with [industry] businesses at your size. We see that a lot."
~"Mmhmm — that's the exact same thing our [similar industry] clients were dealing with before we fixed the follow-up piece."

### The Redirect
When they go off-topic, bring them back without making them feel shut down.

~"Okay okay, that's really helpful — let me grab one more thing before I forget, then we'll circle back to that."
~"Love that — and I want to dig into that more. But real quick before we move on..."

### The Emotional Read
When you hear a shift in energy (frustration, excitement, hesitation), call it out.

~"I can hear the frustration there. Makes total sense."
~"You sound pretty excited about that — tell me more."
~"It sounds like there's some hesitation. What's the concern?"

## PART 4 — CLOSING PSYCHOLOGY

### The ROI Frame (Always Before Price)
Never reveal price before establishing the cost of NOT buying. The prospect should be thinking "I'm already losing $X/month" before they hear any number.

### The "Sound Good" Ladder
Throughout the call, collect 5-7 "sound good" or "yes" or "mmhmm" confirmations. By the time you ask for the appointment or transfer, they've already said yes to you six times. The final yes is just one more.

### The Specific Next Step
Never end with vague. Always end with specific day, specific time, specific action.

**Weak:** "Someone from our team will follow up."
**Strong:** "I'm putting you on the calendar for Thursday at 2pm with Marcus. He'll call you from this same number. Does Thursday at 2 work?"

### The "Anything Else" Silence Bomb
At the end of every call, ask: "Anything I didn't cover that you were expecting me to ask?" Then SHUT UP. Wait a full 5 seconds. Most people fill the silence with their real objection — the one they've been holding back the entire call.

## PART 5 — UNIVERSAL RULES FOR ALL HOOKS

1. **Odd numbers > round numbers.** "43 seconds" not "about a minute." "3 questions" not "a few questions."
2. **Questions > statements.** "How does your team handle follow-up right now?" beats "We handle follow-up better."
3. **Their words > your words.** Mirror their vocabulary within 1-2 turns.
4. **Pain > pleasure.** What they're losing beats what they'd gain. 2x more motivating.
5. **Specific > vague.** "$18K walking out the door" beats "a lot of lost revenue."
6. **Stories > stats.** "We had a client in [industry] who was dealing with the same exact thing. Within 30 days..." beats "Our platform has 95% engagement."
7. **Permission > pushing.** "Would it make sense to..." beats "You should definitely..."
8. **Silence > filling.** After any question, STOP. The prospect who talks more, buys more.
9. **Confirmation > assumption.** Repeat back what they said to prove you listened.
10. **One question at a time. ALWAYS.** This is non-negotiable across every framework.

## HOW TO USE THIS MODULE

This library is a REFERENCE for prompt builders. When building any voice AI agent:

1. **Pick 1-2 openers** from Part 1 that match the use case (speed-to-lead, no-show, nurture, etc.)
2. **Wire the Time Contract** into the opening — always give them a specific time + specific value
3. **Use SPIN** for any qualification section
4. **Use Loss Aversion math** before any booking or transfer attempt
5. **Use the Assumptive Bridge** for the close — never ask IF, ask WHEN/HOW
6. **End with the Silence Bomb** — "Anything I didn't cover?" + shut up

The goal: every person who picks up the phone stays on the phone. Every call feels like talking to the sharpest, most prepared person they've ever met — who also happens to be someone they'd grab a beer with.
