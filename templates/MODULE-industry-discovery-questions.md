# MODULE: Industry-Specific Discovery Questions & Pricing Deflections

> **Reference library.** When generating a voice AI prompt, pull discovery questions from the matching vertical below.
> These replace generic qualification questions with industry-native language that sounds like someone who actually works in that space.
> Source: Closer Level Consultative Sales Scripts (6 verticals)

---

## How to Use This Module

When generating a prompt with `/new-voice-ai-prompt`, identify the client's industry and pull:
1. **Situation questions** — understand their current state
2. **Problem questions** — surface the pain
3. **Consequence questions** — make them feel the cost of inaction
4. **Commitment questions** — test their readiness
5. **Pricing deflection** — how to defer pricing naturally for that industry
6. **Presentation bridge** — the "based on what you shared" transition

Adapt the language to match the agent's character voice (add disfluencies, contractions, reactions per the Character section rules).

## B2B SaaS / Software

**Pricing Deflection:**
~"Pricing depends on team size, use case, and scope, so I'd need to understand your situation first. Is that okay?"

**Situation:**
- Can you walk me through how you're currently handling ___ today?
- How long has that been your setup?
- What tools or systems are involved right now?
- What's working well with your current approach?
- Who else is involved in this process internally?

**Problem:**
- If things are mostly working: If you could improve one thing about how this works today, what would that be?
- If not working well: What's breaking down or becoming a problem?
- What impact is that having on revenue, efficiency, or your team?
- What ultimately pushed you to look for a solution now?

**Consequence:**
- If nothing changes and you keep your current setup, where does that leave you in 6-12 months?
- How does that impact growth targets or team workload?
- Is that an acceptable outcome for you and the business?
- Who ultimately owns the decision if this stays the same or changes?

**Commitment:**
- How high of a priority is it for you to fix this now?
- What happens internally if this doesn't get solved?
- If the right platform existed, would you be open to implementing it properly?

**Presentation Bridge:**
~"Based on what you shared, it sounds like your team wants ___, but right now ___ is creating friction."

~"Based on what you shared, it sounds like you want peace of mind around ___, but right now ___ is causing uncertainty."

## The Universal Consultative Structure (All Verticals)

Every Closer Level script follows this 7-stage flow. When building prompts for industries NOT listed above, use this skeleton and adapt the language:

1. **CONNECTING** — Set expectations, get permission to ask questions, defer pricing naturally
2. **SITUATION** — Understand their current state (5 questions max)
3. **PROBLEM** — Surface the pain, clarify, quantify impact, identify trigger event
4. **SOLUTION AWARENESS** — Have they looked at alternatives? What held them back? What would ideal look like?
5. **CONSEQUENCE** — Cost of inaction (6-12 months), impact on goals, is that acceptable, who decides
6. **COMMITMENT** — Priority level, what happens if not solved, would they act if right solution existed
7. **PRESENTATION** — Mirror their pain back, offer to walk through solution, confirm alignment

**Key patterns across all verticals:**
- Always start with "Can you walk me through..." (open-ended, gives them control)
- Always ask "What made you decide to look into this NOW?" (trigger event = urgency)
- Always ask "Who else is involved in the decision?" (surface the decision-maker early)
- Always ask "Is that acceptable to you?" after painting the consequence (forces them to say no)
- Always end with "Does this feel aligned with what you were hoping to solve?" (trial close)
